Introduction

Problem Statement

  • Longer video content, shorter attention spans.
  • Navigation through streaming videos by scrubbing is slow.
  • Example: Video lectures, seminars and technical presentation videos.
  • Can we do better?

Our Solution

  • Use viewership patterns to find and visually represent interesting sections of video in a heat-map.
  • Challenge: What if insufficient data to generate a heat-map?
    • Generate meaningful navigation points for initial viewers of a particular video.
    • Must not skew results of early users.
  • Use video analysis to find segment boundaries within videos.
  • Use these techniques to generate summaries of streaming video.

Implementation

Goals

  • Make technique available to a large class of streaming video.
  • Allow user constraints on video analysis such as bandwidth and pre-processing time.

Client-side processing

  • Google Chrome Extension.

  • Fetches segment boundaries and heat-map from the backend.

  • Visually presents the relative popularity of various segments of streaming video when available.

  • Performs video analysis on the client using JavaScript.

Video Analysis

  • Perform sampling of video frames for analysis in pre-processing step.
  • resemble.js used to compute grayscale ‘diff’ between successive video frames.
  • Adaptively change sampling frequency (delta) based on previous ‘diff’, bandwidth and time constraints.

Server-side processing

  • RESTful API.
    • Node.js/Express backend.
    • SQLite3 as test DB.
  • Histogram-based computation to determine relative popularity of different video segments.
